# Competitive Ads Extractor

This skill helps you research and analyze competitor advertising to understand their messaging, positioning, and creative strategies.

## When to Use This Skill

- Planning a new ad campaign
- Understanding competitor positioning
- Finding messaging that resonates with your audience
- Researching ad creative trends in your industry
- Preparing competitive analysis for marketing strategy

## What This Skill Does

1. **Identifies Ad Sources**: Guides you to the right ad libraries for each platform
2. **Extracts Ad Data**: Pulls ad copy, visuals, CTAs, and targeting info
3. **Analyzes Patterns**: Identifies common themes, hooks, and frameworks
4. **Benchmarks Performance**: Notes signs of ad performance (running duration, variations)
5. **Generates Insights**: Summarizes actionable takeaways

## Instructions

### Step 1: Identify Competitors and Goals

Ask the user:
- Which competitors to analyze?
- Which platforms? (Facebook/Meta, Google, LinkedIn, TikTok)
- What to learn? (messaging, creative formats, offers, targeting)
- Time period? (recent 30 days, last 3 months)

### Step 2: Access Ad Libraries

Guide user to these free tools:

**Facebook/Instagram/Meta Ads**:
- URL: [Meta Ad Library](https://www.facebook.com/ads/library/)
- Search by: Page name, keyword, category
- Filter: Country, date range, active/inactive

**Google Ads**:
- URL: [Google Ads Transparency Center](https://adstransparency.google.com/)
- Shows: Text and display ads by advertiser

**LinkedIn Ads** (for B2B):
- Visit company page → "Posts" tab → Filter for "Ads"
- LinkedIn Ad Library: [linkedin.com/ad-library](https://www.linkedin.com/ad-library)

**TikTok Ads**:
- URL: [TikTok Creative Center](https://ads.tiktok.com/business/creativecenter/)

**Twitter/X Ads**:
- URL: [X Ads Transparency](https://ads.twitter.com/transparency)

### Step 3: Extract Ad Information

For each ad found, record:

```markdown
## Ad #1
- **Competitor**: [Company Name]
- **Platform**: [Facebook / Google / LinkedIn]
- **Format**: [Image / Video / Carousel / Text]
- **Headline**: [exact headline text]
- **Body Copy**: [full ad copy]
- **CTA**: [Button text - "Sign Up Free", "Learn More", etc.]
- **Offer**: [Free trial / Demo / Discount / Lead magnet]
- **Landing Page**: [URL if visible]
- **Running Since**: [date - longer = likely performing well]
- **Estimated Variations**: [number of versions]
```

### Step 4: Analyze Patterns

After extracting 10-20+ ads per competitor, look for:

**Messaging Patterns**:
- What pain points do they lead with?
- What outcomes/benefits do they promise?
- What's their tone? (urgent, friendly, professional, fear-based)

**Creative Patterns**:
- Image vs. video preference
- Use of social proof (logos, testimonials, numbers)
- Color schemes and visual style
- Text-heavy vs. visual

**Offer Patterns**:
- Pricing strategy (free trial, freemium, demo)
- Lead magnets (ebooks, webinars, tools)
- Discounts and urgency tactics

**Hook Patterns**:
- Question hooks ("Struggling with X?")
- Statistic hooks ("80% of companies...")
- Story hooks ("We went from X to Y")
- Bold claim hooks ("The only tool that...")

### Step 5: Generate Competitive Intelligence Report

```markdown

1. [Pattern with example]

2. [Angle 2 with rationale]
```

## Legal & Ethical Notes

- Only use publicly available ad library data
- Don't copy competitor ads - use for inspiration only
- Do not make false claims about competitors
- Respect intellectual property in creative assets

## Tips

- Ads running for 30+ days are usually profitable - pay close attention
- Multiple ad variations = A/B testing → signals what's working
- Look at landing pages to understand their full funnel
- Track changes over time to spot strategy shifts

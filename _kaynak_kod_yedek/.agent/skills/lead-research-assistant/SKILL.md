# Lead Research Assistant

This skill helps you find, qualify, and prioritize high-quality leads for your product or service by conducting systematic research and building actionable prospect lists.

## When to Use This Skill

- Starting a new sales prospecting campaign
- Finding companies that fit your ideal customer profile (ICP)
- Researching specific prospects before outreach
- Building lead lists for a specific product or feature
- Qualifying inbound leads before investing time

## What This Skill Does

1. **Analyzes Your Product**: Understands what you sell and who benefits most
2. **Defines Ideal Customer Profile**: Builds a precise ICP based on your product
3. **Identifies Target Companies**: Finds businesses that match your ICP
4. **Qualifies Prospects**: Scores based on fit, signals, and timing
5. **Crafts Outreach Strategy**: Personalized messaging per prospect

## Instructions

### Step 1: Understand What You're Selling

Ask the user:
- What is your product/service?
- What problem does it solve?
- Who are your best current customers (if any)?
- What's the price point / deal size?
- What industry/vertical do you focus on?
- What's your sales motion? (inbound, outbound, PLG)

### Step 2: Build the Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

Define the perfect customer:

```markdown
## Ideal Customer Profile

### Company Characteristics
- Industry: [e.g., SaaS companies, ecommerce, healthcare]
- Company size: [e.g., 50-500 employees]
- Revenue: [e.g., $5M-$50M ARR]
- Geography: [e.g., US, Western Europe]
- Tech stack: [e.g., uses Salesforce, AWS, HubSpot]

### Buyer Characteristics  
- Title/Role: [e.g., VP Engineering, Head of Operations]
- Pain points: [e.g., scaling engineering team, reducing churn]
- Buying authority: [budget holder, influencer, user]

### Timing Signals (When they're likely to buy)
- [e.g., Recently raised funding]
- [e.g., Hiring for roles that indicate need]
- [e.g., Job postings mentioning related tools]
- [e.g., Recent product launches]
```

### Step 3: Research Sources

Guide user to these research platforms:
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator** - Company/people search
- **Crunchbase** - Funding data, company info
- **G2 / Capterra** - Companies using competitor tools
- **Apollo.io** - Contact data + intent signals
- **Builtwith.com** - Technology stack data
- **LinkedIn Jobs** - Hiring signals
- **Twitter/X** - Engagement and intent signals

### Step 4: Build Prospect List

For each prospect, research:

```markdown
## Company: [Company Name]
- Website: [URL]
- Size: [employees, revenue if known]
- Industry: [vertical]
- Tech Stack: [relevant tools they use]
- Funding: [latest round, amount, date]
- Key Contact: [name, title, LinkedIn]
- Email: [if available]

## Why They're a Good Fit
- [Specific reason 1]
- [Specific reason 2]

## Buying Signal
- [e.g., Posted 3 DevOps engineer roles this month]
- [e.g., Just raised Series B - $12M]

## Fit Score: [1-10]
## Priority: [High/Medium/Low]
```

### Step 5: Craft Outreach Strategy

For each high-priority prospect:

```markdown
## Outreach Template for [Company]

Subject: [Company] + [Your Company] - [Specific Value Prop]

Hi [First Name],

I noticed [specific signal - funding/job posting/content they shared].

We help companies like [similar company] [solve specific problem].

[One sentence on how you'd specifically help them based on their situation]

Worth a 20-minute call this week?

[Your Name]
```

### Step 6: Prioritize and Score

| Company | Fit Score | Signal Strength | Priority |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|----------|
| Acme Inc | 9/10 | Series B + 5 DevOps hires | High |
| Beta Corp | 7/10 | Hiring + G2 reviews | Medium |

## Tips for Best Results

- Focus depth over breadth - 10 highly researched prospects > 100 generic ones
- Look for "trigger events" (funding, hiring, leadership change) - these indicate active buying
- Personalization is key - reference something specific about each prospect
- Track response rates per segment to refine your ICP over time

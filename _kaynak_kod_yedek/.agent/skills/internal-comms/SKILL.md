# Internal Communications Writer

This skill helps you craft clear, professional internal communications that keep your team informed, aligned, and engaged.

## When to Use This Skill

- Writing weekly/monthly team updates
- Drafting company-wide announcements
- Creating project status reports
- Writing FAQs for internal processes
- Composing executive updates or memos
- Drafting all-hands presentations scripts
- Creating onboarding documentation

## What This Skill Does

1. **Understands Context**: Gathers information about the communication need
2. **Selects Format**: Chooses the right format for the message type
3. **Writes Draft**: Creates professional, clear copy
4. **Adapts Tone**: Matches your company culture (formal, casual, data-driven)
5. **Structures Logically**: Ensures key information flows clearly

## Communication Types

### 3P Update (Progress / Plans / Problems)

```markdown
# [Team/Project] Update - [Date]

## Progress (What we accomplished)
- [Achievement 1 with metrics if possible]
- [Achievement 2]

## Plans (What we're working on next)
- [Priority 1 - target date]
- [Priority 2 - target date]

## Problems (Blockers needing help)
- [Blocker 1] - Need: [specific ask]
- [Blocker 2] - Need: [specific ask]
```

### Project Status Report

```markdown
# [Project Name] - Status Update
**Status**: 🟢 On Track / 🟡 At Risk / 🔴 Off Track
**Date**: [Date]
**Owner**: [Name]

## Summary (2-3 sentences max)
[High-level status for executives who only read this far]

## Key Metrics

|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Launch date | Mar 15 | Mar 15 | 🟢 |
| Budget | $50K | $48K | 🟢 |
| Team capacity | 100% | 85% | 🟡 |

## Accomplishments This Week
- [Item]

## Next Week's Priorities
- [Item]

## Risks & Issues
| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| [Risk] | Medium | High | [Plan] |

## Decisions Needed
- [Decision 1] - Needed by: [Date] - Owner: [Person]
```

### Company Newsletter / All-Hands Update

```markdown

## 🚀 Highlights
[3-5 bullet points of key wins/news]

- [Metric 1]: [Value] ([+/- vs last period])
```

## Instructions

### Step 1: Understand the Communication Need

Ask the user:
- What type of communication? (update, announcement, FAQ, memo)
- Who is the audience? (whole company, specific team, executives, new hires)
- What's the key message or goal?
- What's the tone? (formal, casual, urgent, celebratory)
- Is there a specific template or format required?

### Step 2: Gather the Content

Ask for:
- Key facts, metrics, and data points
- Names of people to mention
- Any links or references to include
- Deadlines or dates
- Any sensitive information to handle carefully

### Step 3: Write the Draft

Apply these principles:
- **Lead with the most important information** (inverted pyramid)
- **Use bullet points** for lists, not long paragraphs
- **Be specific** - use numbers over vague claims
- **Use active voice**: "The team shipped X" not "X was shipped"
- **Keep it scannable** - busy people skim
- **End with clear next steps** or a call to action

### Step 4: Review for Common Mistakes

Check:
- [ ] Is the subject line/title clear and informative?
- [ ] Is the audience clearly identified?
- [ ] Are all facts accurate and verified?
- [ ] Is the ask (if any) explicit?
- [ ] Is the tone appropriate for the audience?
- [ ] Is it too long? (Edit to remove 20%+ if over 300 words)

## Tone Guide

| Audience | Tone | Style |
|----------|------|-------|
| Whole company | Warm, clear | Short sentences, bullets |
| Executives | Crisp, data-driven | Lead with bottom line |
| Engineering | Direct, technical ok | Specifics matter |
| New hires | Welcoming, detailed | Don't assume context |
| External partners | Professional | Formal, no jargon |
